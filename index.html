About

I was born in the Bagerhat district of Bangladesh. Currently, I'm pursuing my PhD in Microbiology at Texas A&M University. I have completed my Master of Science (M.Sc.) and Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) from the Department of Microbiology, Jagannath University, Dhaka 1100, Bangladesh. Currently, I am pursuing my Ph.D. in Microbiology at Texas A&M University. During the Summer 2024 semester, I worked as a Student Research Assistant at Dr. Jared Bard's lab, where I worked on the projects focusing the translational regulation during cellular stress caused by viral pathogens (Chikungunya virus). Previously at Indiana State University, I worked on projects focusing on the c-di-AMP signaling pathway in Group A Streptococcus. My study emphasized the mechanisms governing the virulence components of the Gram-positive human pathogen Streptococcus pyogenes. I have completed my M.Sc. thesis on the "Immunoinformatics approach to epitope-based vaccine design against PspA in Streptococcus pneumoniae and confirmation of the presence of predicted epitope in a plasmid vector" at Bangladesh Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (BCSIR) laboratories, Dhaka. I have a strong desire to continue learning and experiencing new things. My utmost interest and focus on research is host-microbe interaction, bacterial pathogenecity, translational regulation. The function of undiscovered proteins and genes fascinates me, and I'd like to learn more about how these genes confer/aids in the virulence properties of the pathogens and how we can develop inhibitors for these diseases. I want to employ both of my wet laboratory skills and bioinformatics knowledge to do research in these fields. After completing my Ph.D. degree, I want explore my life as a researcher and teacher. I wish to establish a research facility in my motherland, Bangladesh and provide opportunities for all of my country's promising students to participate in research there.
